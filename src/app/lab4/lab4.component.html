Lab question responses

1. The reason of choosing the file format when creating your audio?

I recorded my audio on my phone which saved it as an .m4a format. This format doesn't work with Audacity, so I converted it .FLAC to keep the audio quality as close to the source as possible. Once I made my modifications, I exported the project to .MP3 a lossy format to demonstrate the space saving capabilities of the lossy format.

2. The use of audio on your website regarding usability?

I use audio sparingly on my website, which is a good way to use audio in most cases. For this lab, I implemented audio playback controls to allow the user to play and pause each audio clip as they pleased. I would have liked to add volume controls as well, but time did not permit. I also included front-end code to ensure that no more than one audio clip was playing at a time, which would be disruptive to the user experience.

3. Any thoughts on different audio file formats, e.g., cons, pros, when to use?

In terms of lossless vs. lossy, you would want to use lossless in the cases where the audio really counts, such as production or music playback on high-end audio equipment. With that, comes the cost of large file sizes, which isn't the best for web usability. For the web, you want to use lossy formats where you get away with it, namely where the quality of the audio isn't important to the user in that instance.

In terms of individual formats, aside from format-equipment compatibility requirments, it mostly comes down to preference on the specific advantages of any one format over another.

4. Description of your editing on the file, e.g., cutting, assembly, fadein, fadeout, etc.?

Using Audacity, I applied a reverberation effect, which adds an echo to the sound file, creating lots of individual sound waves that simulate the sound reflecting off of surfaces. Louder sounds in the orginial file will have more of an echo and sound more directional, softer sounds will have less of an echo and sound less directional. After that modfication, I exported the project to .MP3 as opposed to the original FLAC format.
